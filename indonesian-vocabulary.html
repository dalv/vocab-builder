Suggested Additional Categories

Based on your life in Bali, these would be the next high-value lists to build:

  • Discourse particles — sih, dong, kok, deh, lho, ya, kan, nih — these tiny words carry enormous meaning in casual Indonesian and you'll hear them in every single conversation. This might be the single most impactful category to learn next.
  • Common nouns (Bali life) — jalan, pantai, warung, villa, kos, motor, helm, hujan, macet, upacara, pura, sampah, listrik, air, tukang — the physical world around you.
  • Transaction phrases — ordering food, bargaining, asking prices, dealing with laundry/cleaning/villa stuff, getting things fixed, Grab/Gojek interactions.
  • Negation patterns — nggak, bukan, belum, jangan, tanpa — knowing four ways to say "no" is essential.
  • Common verb prefixes/suffixes — me-, di-, ber-, -kan, -in — even a loose grasp of these will unlock your ability to understand speech you overhear.
  • Filler words & reactions — wah, aduh, ya ampun, gitu, gini, emang, masa — the stuff that makes you sound human rather than robotic.
  • Body & health — useful for pharmacies, clinics, describing how you feel (sakit, pusing, demam, batuk, alergi).
